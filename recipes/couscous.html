couscous recipe

Description

Moroccan couscous is an exquisite, emblematic dish that captures the very essence of Moroccan culinary tradition. Made from tiny granules of steamed semolina wheat, it forms the base of a meal that is both comforting and aromatic. Traditionally prepared in a special steamer called a couscoussier, the couscous grains are painstakingly steamed to perfection, ensuring a light and fluffy texture. Atop these delicate grains, a richly flavored stew is poured, teeming with tender chunks of lamb, beef, or chicken, and an assortment of root vegetables like carrots, turnips, and zucchini. Spiced with a unique blend of coriander, saffron, ginger, and cinnamon, among others, this dish is given its characteristic warmth and depth. To add color, texture, and a sweet contrast, one might also find caramelized onions, raisins, and chickpeas interspersed. Garnished with fresh cilantro or parsley, Moroccan couscous is more than just a meal; it's a harmonious symphony of flavors, textures, and traditions, presented on a single plate.

Ingredients

For the Couscous:

  • 2 cups of couscous (preferably the traditional Moroccan variety, not the instant type)
  • 3 cups of water (for steaming)
  • 2 tablespoons of extra-virgin olive oil or butter
  • Salt to taste

For the Stew:

  • Meat of your choice: lamb, chicken, or beef, cut into chunks
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 1 turnip, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup of chickpeas (soaked overnight if using dried, or canned)
  • 2 tomatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1/2 cup of raisins (optional)
  • A pinch of saffron threads (or turmeric for color)
  • 1 teaspoon of ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4-5 cups of water or broth
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • 2 tablespoons of extra-virgin olive oil or clarified butter (for frying and stewing)

Optional:

  • Other vegetables like bell peppers, cabbage, or pumpkin
  • Harissa sauce or chili paste for added heat

Steps

Preparing the Couscous:

  1. Rinse and Hydrate: Begin by rinsing the couscous in cold water to remove any excess starch. Drain it and then let it sit for about 20 minutes to slightly hydrate.
  2. Fluffing: After hydrating, gently break apart any clumps with your fingers and mix in a bit of olive oil or butter. This helps in ensuring the grains remain separate and fluffy.
  3. Steam: Traditionally, couscous is steamed over the stew using a couscoussier. Fill the bottom part with the meat and vegetables stew, and place the couscous in the top section to steam. If you don't have a couscoussier, you can use a regular steamer. Steam the couscous for about 15-20 minutes.
  4. Second Steam: Remove the couscous from the steamer, fluff again and let it cool a bit. Steam a second time for another 15 minutes until it's fully cooked and fluffy.

Preparing the Stew:

  1. Brown the Meat: In a large pot or the bottom part of the couscoussier, heat the oil or clarified butter. Add the meat chunks and brown them on all sides.
  2. Aromatics: Add the finely chopped onions and minced garlic to the pot. Sauté until they are translucent and aromatic.
  3. Spices and Vegetables: Introduce the spices—ginger, cinnamon, cumin, coriander, saffron (or turmeric), salt, and pepper. Stir well before adding the hard vegetables like carrots and turnips.
  4. Liquids: Pour in the water or broth, ensuring that the meat and vegetables are submerged. Bring to a boil and then reduce to a simmer.
  5. Simmering: Cover the pot and let the stew simmer until the meat is halfway cooked. This might take 30 to 40 minutes depending on the meat's type and cut.
  6. Additional Vegetables: Add the softer vegetables like zucchini, tomatoes, and chickpeas, and continue to simmer. If using raisins, they can be added at this stage.
  7. Finalizing the Stew: Once the meat and vegetables are tender and the stew is flavorful, adjust the seasoning if necessary.

Combining the Couscous and Stew:

  1. Serving: Transfer the steamed couscous to a large serving dish. Make a well in the center and ladle the meat and vegetables into this well. Pour some broth over the couscous, ensuring it's moist but not soggy.
  2. Garnish: Finish off by garnishing with fresh cilantro or parsley.
