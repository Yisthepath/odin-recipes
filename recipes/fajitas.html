Fajitas recipe

Description

Fajitas are a sizzling Mexican dish primarily consisting of grilled marinated meat, typically beef or chicken, served alongside sautéed bell peppers and onions. Originally hailing from the ranchlands of Texas, this flavorful concoction is traditionally served with warm flour tortillas. Complementing the main ingredients are a variety of accompaniments such as guacamole, sour cream, pico de gallo, and shredded cheese. When assembled to one's preference, the combination creates a delightful medley of textures and tastes that dance on the palate. Whether enjoyed in a bustling cantina or a cozy kitchen, fajitas are a festive treat that promises a burst of Tex-Mex flavor with every bite.

Ingredients

For the chicken:

  • 1 to 1.5 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, thinly sliced

For the marinade:

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 1-2 limes, juiced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (for added heat)

For the vegetables:

  • 1 large red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 large green bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 large yellow onion, thinly sliced

For serving:

  • Flour tortillas (you can also use corn tortillas if preferred)
  • Guacamole
  • Sour cream
  • Pico de gallo or salsa
  • Shredded cheese (e.g., Monterey Jack, cheddar, or a blend)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)
  • Lime wedges

Optional toppings:

  • Sliced jalapeños
  • Sliced avocados
  • Refried beans
  • Lettuce, shredded

Steps

Preparation

  1. Wash and pat dry the chicken. Slice it into thin strips.
  2. Slice the bell peppers and onions into thin strips.

Marinating the Chicken

  1. In a bowl, mix together olive oil, lime juice, minced garlic, ground cumin, chili powder, paprika, salt, pepper, and optionally, crushed red pepper flakes.
  2. Add the sliced chicken to the bowl, ensuring all pieces are well-coated with the marinade.
  3. Allow the chicken to marinate for at least 30 minutes to an hour in the refrigerator. For best results, marinate for 2-3 hours.

Cooking the Vegetables

  1. In a large skillet or frying pan, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the sliced bell peppers and onions, sautéing until they are soft and slightly caramelized, which should take around 6-8 minutes.
  3. Once cooked, remove the vegetables from the skillet and set them aside.

Cooking the Chicken

  1. Place the marinated chicken strips into the skillet, ensuring they are spread out and not overcrowded.
  2. Cook the chicken for about 5-7 minutes or until fully cooked through and slightly browned on the outside. Stir occasionally to ensure even cooking.
  3. Once cooked, you can return the vegetables to the skillet and toss them with the chicken, or keep them separate based on your presentation preference.
  4. In the same skillet, add a bit more oil if necessary.

Warming the Tortillas

  1. Using a clean skillet or comal, warm each tortilla for about 20-30 seconds on each side or until soft and pliable.
  2. Alternatively, you can wrap a stack of tortillas in aluminum foil and warm them in a preheated oven (350°F or 175°C) for 10-15 minutes.

Serving

  1. To assemble a fajita, take a warm tortilla, add some of the chicken and vegetable mixture, and top with your desired toppings such as guacamole, sour cream, pico de gallo, shredded cheese, and more.
  2. Lay out all the toppings and accompaniments.
  3. Serve immediately with lime wedges on the side for an added zesty flavor.

Enjoyment

  1. Enjoy your fajitas!
